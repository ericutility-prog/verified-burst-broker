∎ the part that's actually hard

Charging for AI correctness is a wallet-drain waiting to happen: feed the system answers engineered to fail verification and you burn its judge tokens for free, forever. So the free-miss allowance is lifetime-cumulative and consumable — settling a cheap passing call can't reset it — which bounds an attacker's free burn to a function of their own settled spend. We found that exact reset-the-ceiling ratchet in our own adversarial audit and closed it before shipping.

the guarantees, stated plainly
∎

Decorrelated verification

The judge runs on a different model family and vendor than the generator — the one check an agent can't self-supply from its own correlated samples.

∎

Settle-then-deliver

The on-chain payment is independently confirmed to have moved the fee before the result is released. A lying or buggy facilitator buys nothing.

∎

Fail-closed

A dead or erroring judge withholds the answer and charges nothing — never a silent pass dressed up as a verdict.

∎

Measured, not asserted

Verifier false-confirm and false-alarm rates are benchmarked on programmatic ground truth — the catch rate can't be cooked.

Small and early by design — this is a correctness-and-safety guarantee you can read the code for, not a claim about scale.
